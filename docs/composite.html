Forensic composite profile
Subject: Unknown Biped

A composite morphological and behavioural profile constructed from 121 witness accounts, weighted by Witness Credibility Score. Every descriptor sourced to a specific percentage of the corpus. No embellishment. No interpretation. Only what witnesses reported.

This is not an artistic impression. It is a data document. The silhouette is a schematic based on the most frequently reported proportional features — not a rendering of something observed.

How this profile was constructed: Each feature below is drawn from structured extraction of 121 witness accounts, weighted by Witness Credibility Score (WCS ≥ 7.0 = Strong). Percentages reflect the proportion of accounts in which a feature was explicitly described — not the proportion in which it was absent. Absence of description does not imply absence of feature. Features reported in fewer than 15% of accounts are listed under "Low consensus / contested." No features have been inferred, extrapolated, or added by the researchers.
Physical — Morphology
Bipedal locomotion 83% of accounts
Upright, two-legged. Described as "walking like a man" but with animal proportions.
Height 7–8 feet most frequent range
Range across corpus: 6–10ft. Median estimate 7.5ft. Consistently described as significantly taller than an adult human.
Canine head type dominant description
Dog or wolf-like face, elongated snout, upright ears. Occasionally described as "wolf" or "German Shepherd" specifically.
Muscular build consistent across accounts
Prominent haunches. Wide chest. Described variously as "600–700 pounds," "muscular like a horse," "very thick and wide."
Also observed quadrupedal 46% of accounts
Switches between upright and four-legged movement. Often drops to all fours when departing at speed.
"It had a dog face, clearly had the dog's ears, but they were very tufted, more like a cat. The fur on the neck was slightly longer."

Sensory — Smell & Sound
Smell reported 27% of accounts
Consistently described as putrid, rotten, or intensely animal. "Wet dog and death." "Rotten flesh." "Curdled cheese and rancid fat." Not one account describes a neutral or pleasant smell.
"The smell that hit me as it stood up was this really musky — like it smelled like a wet dog and death."
WCS 8.2
Infrasound felt in chest subset of accounts
Low-frequency growl felt physically rather than heard audibly. Described as vibration in the chest, seat of the car, or ground beneath the witness.
"The growl was so low that audibly I could barely hear it, but I could feel it in my chest. I could feel it in the seat of the car."
WCS 8.2
Vocalisation type
Growl (dominant), howl (some), and — notably — silence. A significant proportion of accounts describe the entity making no sound at all, including during rapid movement through dense vegetation.
Extremities
Hands described 60% of accounts
Described as hand-like rather than paw-like. Long fingers. Claws described as "made for killing" and "dagger-like." Several accounts compare them to raccoon hands — long-fingered, clearly articulated.
"It opened up its hands and they were clearly hands — and they had claws on the end of them that were definitely made for killing."
WCS 8.2
"Overgrown raccoon hands. But it had like four long fingers with dagger-like claws. I would say they were probably three to four inches long."

Behavioural Signature
Watches then leaves
50%
Observes the witness for a sustained period — seconds to minutes — then withdraws. Does not attack. Does not flee in panic. Deliberate departure.
Silent departure
40%
Leaves without sound — even through vegetation that should produce noise. Witnesses frequently describe being unable to hear movement after the entity withdraws.
Witness paralysis
40%
Involuntary physical immobility during the encounter. Not a choice not to move — described as inability to move. Resolves after entity departs.
Pre-encounter silence
subset
All ambient sound — insects, birds, wind — stops before the entity appears. Noted across geographically and temporally separate accounts with no contact between witnesses.
Nocturnal / low-light
dominant
Night, dusk, or dawn in the majority of accounts. Daylight encounters exist but are a minority. Forest and wooded terrain in over 90% of accounts.
Felt known by entity
subset
Witnesses describe a distinct sense that the entity was aware of them specifically — not a general reaction to a human presence. Some describe sustained eye contact without aggression.
Low consensus / Contested features

Features reported in fewer than 15% of accounts, or where accounts significantly contradict each other. Included for completeness — not part of the core composite profile.

TailSome accounts describe a bushy tail; others describe none. No consensus.
Semi-transparent / cloaked appearanceReported in a small number of accounts. Cannot be reconciled with majority description.
White or light-coloured fur2% of accounts. Not part of core profile but documented.
Multiple individuals sighted simultaneouslyRare but present. Several accounts describe pairs or small groups.
Humanoid faceA small number describe primate-like or human-like facial features rather than purely canine. Low frequency.
Missing timeReported in a minority of accounts. Time gap between encounter and next clear memory. No explanation offered by witnesses.
Researcher note: This composite represents the current state of the corpus at N=121. It will be updated as the corpus grows. High-frequency features (≥50% of accounts) are considered core descriptors. Features between 15–50% are secondary. Below 15% is listed as contested. The composite does not assert that an entity matching this description exists. It asserts only that this is what 121 people, evaluated for credibility, described seeing.
