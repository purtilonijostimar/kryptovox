# Kryptovox — Project Vision
**Founded:** March 2026
**Author:** Urh Ferlež | **System:** Jonek

---

## What This Is

Kryptovox is the world's first structured academic database of anomalous bipedal creature encounter testimony — cross-referenced against global mythology, folklore, and historical record spanning thousands of years.

The name comes from Greek *kryptos* (hidden) and Latin *vox* (voice). Hidden voices — the dismissed witnesses, the ancient storytellers, the cultures that encoded something into mythology and were never taken seriously.

We take them seriously.

---

## The Core Insight

Modern AI tools and an unprecedented volume of recorded witness testimony have created a unique historical moment. For the first time, it is possible to:

1. Collect hundreds of hours of structured first-person encounter accounts
2. Transcribe, tag, and analyse them at scale
3. Cross-reference physical descriptors against each other and against ancient records
4. Identify statistical patterns invisible to a casual listener

The question we ask is not *"does this creature exist?"*

The question we ask is: **"Why does this description persist — consistently, independently, across cultures with no apparent contact — across thousands of years?"**

That question is scientifically legitimate regardless of the answer.

## The Two Primary Phenomena

### Dogman / Bipedal Canine
- Modern testimony corpus: Dogman Encounters (Vic Cundiff), 500+ hours
- Historical record: Anubis (Egypt), Fenrir (Norse), Cynocephali (Greek/Roman), Wild Hunt (pan-European), werewolf folklore (global)
- Slovenian connection: volkodlak tradition, forest spirit encounters in local oral history
- Recurring physical descriptors: self-illuminated eyes, 7–8ft height, bipedal gait, canine facial structure, musk/sulphur smell

### Bigfoot / Bipedal Ape
- Modern testimony corpus: BFRO database, multiple YouTube channels
- Historical record: Yeti (Himalayan), Yowie (Australia), Almas (Central Asia), Sasquatch (Pacific Northwest indigenous tradition), Wildman accounts globally
- Recurring physical descriptors: 8–10ft height, brown/black hair, footprints 15–18", infrasound, eye shine, tree knocking

### Cross-Phenomenon Questions
- Do the two phenomena share behavioural patterns (approach behaviour, witness emotional response, geographic preference)?
- Do they share the same geographic hotspots or occupy distinct territories?
- Do ancient cultures that recorded one also record the other?

## Research Layers

### Layer 1 — The Modern Testimony Corpus
Transcribe, structure, and analyse the full available audio/video record.

**Per-account extraction schema:**
- Witness ID (anonymised), approximate age, profession, location (state/country/terrain type)
- Date of encounter (approximate), time of day, season
- Duration of encounter
- Physical descriptors: height estimate, build, hair/fur colour, face type, eye characteristics (glow Y/N, colour), smell, sound produced
- Behavioural descriptors: approach type, movement pattern, reaction to witness, threat display Y/N
- Witness response: freeze, flee, weapon drawn, vehicle, emotional state
- Emotional markers in testimony: voice break, crying, long pause, refusal to continue, minimisation
- Account source: channel, episode number, date published

### Layer 2 — The Historical Record
Curated database of ancient and medieval accounts, organised by:
- Culture / geography
- Time period
- Physical descriptors (mapped to same schema as Layer 1 where possible)
- Context (religious, folkloric, eyewitness, second-hand)

**Priority sources:**
- Egyptian funerary texts (Anubis, Wepwawet)
- Norse Eddas (Fenrir, úlfhéðinn)
- Greek/Roman sources (Cynocephali, Pliny the Elder)
- Medieval European werewolf trial records (genuine court documents)
- Slovenian ethnographic collections (ZRC SAZU archive, Štrekelj's folk poetry collection)
- Pacific Northwest indigenous oral tradition (Lkwungen, Squamish, Nlaka'pamux)
- Himalayan expedition accounts (Shipton footprint, Hillary)

### Layer 3 — The Intersection (The Finding)
Where Layer 1 and Layer 2 overlap is the research finding.

If a truck driver in rural Michigan in 2019 independently describes the same self-illuminated eyes, the same proportions, the same tree-line behaviour as a Slovenian village legend from the 16th century — without any plausible cultural transmission between them — that is the paper.

## The Slovenian Thread

A dedicated research chapter focusing on:
- Identified Slovenian folkloric entities that match Dogman/Bigfoot descriptors
- Geographic hotspots within Slovenia (terrain mapping)
- ZRC SAZU (Research Centre of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences) ethnographic archives
- Potential outreach to Slovenian folklorists and ethnologists
- Personal fieldwork possibility (interviewing elderly rural witnesses)

This is the personal anchor of the project and gives it a specific, verifiable geographic lens that strengthens the methodology.

---

## The Emotional Authenticity Thread

A dedicated analytical chapter on witness testimony markers:

Research question: *Do Kryptovox accounts show emotional patterns consistent with genuine trauma testimony, and are those patterns inconsistent with fabricated accounts?*

Method: Tag every emotional break, pause, minimisation ("I know how this sounds"), refusal to continue, and crying event. Map against narrative moment (first sighting, eye contact, creature vocalisation, creature departure).

Cross-reference against trauma testimony research in psychology literature (PTSD verbal markers, credibility indicators used in forensic interviewing).

This chapter does not require the creature to be real. It asks whether the *testimony* is consistent with genuine traumatic experience — which is a legitimate and answerable question.

## Output

1. **Research paper** — academic format, submittable to journals covering anomalous phenomena, folklore studies, or consciousness/perception research
2. **Interactive map** — geographic distribution of accounts, filterable by creature type, descriptor, time period
3. **Kryptovox database** — publicly accessible structured dataset (anonymised)
4. **Kryptovox.com** — public-facing site presenting findings accessibly

---

## What We Are Not Doing

- We are not claiming the creatures exist
- We are not sensationalising or entertaining
- We are not debunking — dismissal without analysis is as unscientific as credulity
- We are not building a fan site

We are doing what should have been done decades ago: treating a large, consistent body of witness testimony with the same rigour applied to any other phenomenon.
