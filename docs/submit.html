Your experience matters to the research

We are building the largest systematically analysed corpus of anomalous encounter testimony ever assembled. Every vetted account adds to the statistical picture.

What happens to your submission

  • 1 We review your submission for completeness and basic consistency
  • 2 Approved accounts are transcribed, extracted, and scored through our full analytical pipeline
  • 3 Your account is published on the public dashboard with your WCS score and findings
  • 4 Your data contributes to the statistical analysis in the working paper
  • 5 You can remain fully anonymous — we publish the account, not your identity
All submissions are reviewed before entering the pipeline. We are looking for genuine accounts — not perfect ones. Uncertainty, fragmented memory, and emotional difficulty are signs of authentic recall, not reasons for rejection. Accounts that appear deliberately fabricated or that contain demonstrable inconsistencies will not be published.

What makes a strong submission

You don't need to have had a dramatic encounter. Partial encounters, ambiguous sightings, and accounts where you're not sure what you saw are all valuable. What matters is honest, detailed recall.

Accounts that score well tend to include:

  • → Specific sensory detail across multiple channels (what you saw, heard, smelled, felt)
  • → Context before the encounter — what you were doing, why you were there
  • → Uncertainty and self-correction — "I think it was..." or "I'm not sure but..."
  • → How it affected you afterwards — did you tell anyone? Did it change your behaviour?
  • → Why you're coming forward now — the disclosure story matters as much as the encounter
