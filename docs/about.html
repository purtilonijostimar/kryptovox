About the project
Rigorous research into a phenomenon that deserves it

The researcher

Purtilonij Ostimar is a creative professional with nearly a decade of independent research into anomalous phenomena. His work began with pattern recognition — noticing, across vast bodies of witness testimony now accessible through decades of internet archives, that something consistent was being described by people with no connection to each other.

He approaches the subject empirically, without predetermined conclusions. After years of observation he arrived at a position he describes simply: something is going on. What that something is remains the question.

The official acknowledgement of UAP — unidentified aerial phenomena — by governments and military agencies in recent years matters here. If one category of phenomenon long dismissed as impossible has proven real, the framework for confidently dismissing all others deserves scrutiny. The door, once opened, opens onto a great deal.

Kryptovox is his attempt to find out what else might be on the other side — rigorously, transparently, and at a scale that has not been attempted before.

The project

Kryptovox is a long-term independent research initiative applying forensic credibility analysis to anomalous encounter testimony at corpus scale. We do not assert that the described phenomena exist. We assert that the testimony deserves serious analytical attention — and that the tools to give it that attention now exist.

The primary corpus is the Dogman Encounters podcast archive — over 600 long-form witness interviews conducted by a single interviewer over more than a decade. We transcribe, extract, score, and analyse each account using a fully documented, replicable pipeline.

Core principles

No predetermined conclusions

We do not set out to prove or disprove. We set out to measure. The data leads.

Full transparency

All methodology, prompts, and scoring rubrics are documented and open-source. Replicate us. Challenge us.

Witness dignity

Every account represents a real person describing a real experience. We treat it accordingly.

Measured claims

We report what the data shows. Nothing more. The reader draws their own conclusions.

The pipeline

Every account in the corpus passes through the same analytical process:

1
Transcription
Whisper large-v3 via Groq API — segment-level timestamps, ~100× realtime processing speed.
2
Structured extraction
80+ fields per account extracted by Claude — morphology, behaviour, witness background, community dynamics, anomaly assessment.
3
Credibility scoring (WCS)
Six-dimension score derived from CBCA and SVA forensic frameworks. Each score includes rationale and evidence quotes.
4
Acoustic analysis
Prosodic features extracted with librosa — pitch, volume, pace, pauses, tremor mapped to transcript timestamps.
5
Pattern analysis & publication
Descriptor frequencies, temporal stability, credibility correlation. Results published in the dashboard and working paper.

Open source

The full pipeline — download, transcription, extraction, scoring, acoustic analysis, and dashboard export — is available on GitHub. We encourage researchers to fork it, apply it to other corpora, and improve it.
