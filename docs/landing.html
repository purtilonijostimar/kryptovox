What we found
01
The accounts hold up under scrutiny
Applying six-dimension forensic credibility scoring derived from criminal testimony analysis, the majority of accounts show structural markers consistent with genuine traumatic memory — not fabricated narrative.
02
The unusual details are stable across time
Specific anatomical and behavioural descriptors appear at consistent rates in accounts predating widespread internet discussion of the phenomenon. Social contagion does not explain their distribution.
03
History has been describing this for centuries
The same behavioural markers appear in Slovenian folklore, Mayan tradition, Navajo oral history, and Siberian shamanic accounts — independently, with no plausible transmission route between them and modern witnesses.
"Either this represents an extraordinary concentration of skilled fabricators with nothing to gain — or the descriptors reflect genuine perceptual experience of something not yet catalogued by mainstream science."
— From the working paper
The methodology

How we analyse testimony

01
Transcription
Every interview is transcribed with word-level timestamps using Whisper large-v3, producing a searchable, analysable text record of the spoken account.
02
Structured extraction
80+ fields extracted per account — creature morphology, witness background, behavioural markers, physiological responses, light anomalies, and community dynamics.
03
Credibility scoring
Six-dimension Witness Credibility Score derived from forensic psychology tools used in criminal testimony analysis. Each score includes rationale and evidence quotes.
04
Acoustic analysis
Prosodic features — pitch, volume, pace, pauses, tremor — extracted at the segment level. Emotional response mapped to specific moments in the narrative.
05
Pattern analysis
Descriptor frequencies tested for statistical significance, temporal stability, and correlation with credibility scores. Cross-referenced against historical and folklore records.
06
Open science
Full pipeline documented and open-source. All prompts, scoring rubrics, and methodology available for replication, challenge, and refinement by other researchers.
