Research roadmap
Where this is going

Milestone-driven. No artificial deadlines. Each phase unlocks when the previous one is complete and validated.

1
In Progress
Phase 1
Foundation

Complete analysis of the Dogman Encounters corpus. Prove the methodology. Publish the first working paper. Establish the public research dashboard. This phase validates every analytical tool before we scale.

  • 600+ Dogman Encounters episodes transcribed and analysed
  • 100-account milestone review — schema refinement, methodology validation
  • Full statistical analysis: descriptor frequencies, WCS distribution, pre/post-internet stability
  • Acoustic emotional timeline — cross-account pattern analysis
  • Working paper v1.0 submitted to peer-reviewed journal
  • Public dashboard live with full corpus
  • Cross-cultural folklore research documented
2
Next
Phase 2
Platform

Open the submission portal. Move beyond a single channel. Build the infrastructure for a self-sustaining, growing corpus of vetted witness accounts from around the world.

  • Public submission portal — audio, video, or written accounts
  • Documented vetting process — transparent criteria, consistent application
  • First externally submitted accounts added to corpus
  • Non-English account support — transcription and extraction in multiple languages
  • Community structure analysis — referral patterns, disclosure dynamics
  • Dashboard updated to show source pool (Dogman Encounters vs Submitted)
3
Future
Phase 3
Expansion

Add three additional high-quality witness interview channels to the corpus. Cross-channel analysis. Does the same methodology produce consistent findings across different interviewers and audiences?

  • 1,000+ total accounts across multiple channels
  • Cross-channel WCS comparison — does interviewer style affect scoring?
  • Descriptor frequency stability across channels
  • Second peer-reviewed paper — expanded corpus findings
  • Academic consultants — ethnographer, forensic psychologist review
4
Future
Phase 4
Bigfoot Chapter

Apply the same methodology to Sasquatch/Bigfoot testimony. Are the WCS distributions similar? Do the unusual descriptors overlap or diverge? Is this one phenomenon or two? The comparative analysis is where the research gets genuinely interesting.

  • Sasquatch Chronicles and BFRO corpus added
  • Side-by-side WCS comparison — dogman vs bigfoot credibility profiles
  • Descriptor overlap analysis — what do they share, what diverges
  • Slovenian Divji mož (Wild Man) folklore — Bigfoot parallel documented
  • Third peer-reviewed paper — comparative analysis
5
Future
Phase 5
Open Science

Release everything. Full dataset. Full pipeline. Full methodology. This research belongs to anyone who wants to use it, challenge it, or build on it.

  • Full anonymised dataset released under open licence
  • Pipeline open-sourced and documented for external use
  • Conference presentation
  • Book proposal — narrative account of the research and its findings
  • Collaboration with independent researchers applying the methodology to other corpora
